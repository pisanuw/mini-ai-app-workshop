Building with AI

A 60-minute, building-heavy session for about 10 adults. Goal: everyone leaves having made at least one working web app, and knowing how to make more. Success is emotional as much as technical, so keep it warm, unhurried on setup, and celebratory.

The three files

  • ai-workshop-deck.html — project this (or host it on Netlify and open the link). Put your name and date on the title slide first. Arrow keys or the corner buttons to advance.
  • participant-handout.html — print one per person; leave on the tables.
  • facilitator-guide.html — this page. Do not hand out.

Before you start

  • Mirror your laptop to the screen; check the back row can read the slides.
  • Confirm wifi works. Have a phone hotspot as backup.
  • Ask everyone to sign up at claude.ai before arriving, and keep the first 5 minutes to help stragglers log in.
  • If some have no device or account, plan to pair them with a neighbor who does.
  • Open the deck full-screen; edit the title slide with your name and date.
  • Print the handout, one per seat.
  • Do a dry run of your live demo once, so you know exactly what appears.
  • Keep 2 to 3 rescue prompts ready for anyone stuck (see below).

Your live-demo script

Paste thisFor a group of friends, build me a web app that splits a restaurant bill and the tip. Let me type the total, the tip percent, and the number of people, then show what each person owes in big, clear numbers. Cheerful, warm colors.

When it appears, name what you see. Then show one tweak:

Then paste thisNow add a button that rounds each person up to the nearest dollar.

Point back at the recipe slide: "Who it's for, what it does, details, how it looks, build it." Aim for 8 minutes total. Do not gold-plate it.

Rescue prompts

Hand these to anyone frozen, to get them a quick win. (The handout menu has more.)

  • Countdown: "Build me a web app that counts down the days to a date I choose. Big friendly text, cheerful colors."
  • Kind message: "Build me a web app that shows a warm, encouraging message each time I press a button. Soft, pretty colors."
  • Dice / picker: "Build me a web app with a big button that picks a random number from 1 to 6 and shows it large."

Facilitation tips for this group

  • Budget generous time for setup. Login friction is the number one time-sink; front-load it.
  • Pair the nervous with the confident. Peer help scales better than you running around.
  • Celebrate every single result out loud. Momentum here is emotional, not technical.
  • Say "you can't break it" early and often. Fear of breaking things is the real blocker.
  • Screenshots are fine. Nobody needs to save or understand the code.
  • Resist teaching everything. Deep question? "Great rainy-day question," and point to the handout.
  • Slow down your clicking and speaking. Read each slide aloud.

Troubleshooting

Can't log in: use the Claude app on their phone as a fallback, or pair them up.
Blank or odd output: "just ask again," or "tell it what you actually wanted." Re-asking fixes most things.
"Mine looks different from hers": that is normal and good. Every build varies a little.
Someone races ahead: point them at a Medium app or the make-it-weirder round.
Deck won't advance: use the arrow keys, or refresh the page.
